## Competitor Deep Dives

### 1. Trace (traceup.com)

**What they do:** Automated video capture using a physical camera (TraceCam). AI generates highlights, player heatmaps, and individual analytics for any position.

**Pricing:**
- PlayerFocus Basic: $25/mo or $180/yr
- PlayerFocus Pro: $35/mo or $300/yr
- Camera: ~$99 lease or included in subscription

**Strengths:**
- Strong brand recognition in youth soccer
- Excellent video automation (no manual filming)
- Team-level analytics well-established

**Weaknesses:**
- Requires hardware (TraceCam) — massive friction for individual coaches
- NOT goalkeeper-specific — treats GK like any other position
- No AI coaching report cards
- No age-appropriate development framework
- Expensive relative to what GK coaches get from it

**Our angle against Trace:**
> "Trace tells you what happened. My Keeper Coach tells you what to do about it — specifically for your goalkeeper, at their age."

**Keyword opportunities vs. Trace:**
- "goalkeeper coaching app without hardware"
- "goalkeeper development tracker alternatives to Trace"
- "AI goalkeeper report card"

---

### 2. The Keeper Club (keeperwarsink.com)

**What they do:** Goalkeeper coaching membership — training videos, fitness content, community, and a fitness app with leaderboard.

**Pricing:**
- Monthly: $19.99/mo
- Annual: $210/yr (~$17.50/mo)

**Strengths:**
- GK-specific identity and community
- Wide content library
- Affordable

**Weaknesses:**
- No individual keeper tracking or development data
- Content consumption model — passive, not coach-driven
- No AI or personalization
- Not coach-facing — aimed at the goalkeeper themselves

**Our angle against Keeper Club:**
> "The Keeper Club gives goalkeepers content to watch. My Keeper Coach gives coaches a system to develop."

---

### 3. Hudl (hudl.com)

**What they do:** Industry-standard team video platform for high school, college, and semi-pro soccer. Film breakdown, plays, stats.

**Pricing:**
- Team packages from $2,000+/yr
- Individual player spotlight products also available

**Strengths:**
- Dominant brand — coaches know it
- Comprehensive for older/higher-level teams
- Robust editing and tagging tools

**Weaknesses:**
- Built for teams, not individual player/GK development
- Expensive — completely out of reach for youth GK coaches
- No age-appropriate framework
- No AI coaching output specific to goalkeepers
- Heavy — designed for professional video staff

**Our angle against Hudl:**
> "Hudl is for the coaching staff at your high school. My Keeper Coach is for the goalkeeper coach who shows up with 3 keepers and a phone."

---

### 4. TeamSnap (teamsnap.com)

**What they do:** Team logistics, scheduling, roster management, communication.

**Pricing:**
- Free (basic)
- Premium: $9.99/mo or $99.99/yr
- Ultra: $17.99/mo or $199.99/yr

**Strengths:**
- Very widely adopted in youth sports
- Strong parent communication features
- Easy to use

**Weaknesses:**
- Zero goalkeeper development specificity
- No video analysis or AI
- Logistics tool, not a development tool
- Generic — not designed to make keepers better

**Our angle against TeamSnap:**
> "TeamSnap tells parents when practice is. My Keeper Coach tells them how their goalkeeper is growing."

---

### 5. Coachbetter (coachbetter.com)

**What they do:** Session planning software for soccer coaches — design training sessions, access drill library.

**Pricing:**
- Base: $7/mo or $65/yr
- Plus: $13/mo or $120/yr
- Pro: ~$160/yr

**Strengths:**
- Affordable
- Good session design tools
- Drill library

**Weaknesses:**
- No video analysis or AI
- Not goalkeeper-specific
- No development tracking over time
- No parent-facing features

**Our angle against Coachbetter:**
> "Coachbetter plans the session. My Keeper Coach tracks whether it worked."
