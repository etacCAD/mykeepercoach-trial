# My Keeper Coach — Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

> Defines the 3 primary buyer/user personas. All GTM skills use this to target the right people, write the right copy, and find them in the right places.

---

## ICP 1: The Dedicated Club GK Coach (Primary Buyer)

**Nick, 38. GK Coach at a PREMIER/ECNL club.**

### Demographics
- Age: 28–52
- Gender: 70% male, 30% female
- Location: Suburban US, near a metro soccer hub
- Income: Middle to upper-middle class. Either volunteers or earns $20–60/hr coaching.
- Former goalkeeper themselves in 60% of cases

### Situation
- Coaches 1–3 teams worth of keepers (4–12 keepers total)
- Works a full-time job. Coaches evenings and weekends.
- Genuinely loves goalkeeper development but has no structured tools
- Writes notes in a notebook or doesn't write notes at all
- Parent questions after games stress him out — he knows the keeper is improving but can't prove it

### Pain Points
- "I have no way to show a parent what's actually getting better."
- "Every keeper gets 20 minutes of shots at the end of practice and it feels like I'm failing them."
- "I have to re-learn every keeper from scratch each season because my notes are a mess."
- "I'd love to give individualized feedback but I don't have time to write 8 reports after every session."

### Dreams
- Be known as the coach whose keepers actually develop
- Have parents trust him completely
- Be more professional — have a real system
- Get keepers into high school or college programs and feel proud of the role he played

### Where to Find Him
- **Instagram:** Follows goalkeeper coaching accounts (@keepercoachacademy, @gkspecialist, @usa_goalkeeper)
- **Facebook Groups:** "Youth Soccer Coaches" groups, state association pages
- **YouTube:** Searches "goalkeeper drills U13," "teaching the collapse dive"
- **United Soccer Coaches:** Member or aspires to be
- **Reddit:** r/bootroom, r/soccercoaching
- **Local:** Club training sessions, soccer tournaments, coaching clinics

### How He Discovers Products
- Word of mouth from other coaches at tournaments (most powerful)
- Instagram ad or organic Reel that shows a real GK report card
- Google: "goalkeeper coaching app" or "how to track goalkeeper development"
- Recommended in a coaching Facebook group post

### Decision Criteria
- "Does this actually save me time?"
- "Will parents see real value in this?"
- "Is this built for goalkeepers or just adapted from something else?"
- Price: Will spend $15–25/mo without much friction if the value is clear

## ICP 2: The Private Goalkeeper Trainer (High-Value Buyer)

**Sarah, 29. Private GK trainer — runs her own training business.**

### Demographics
- Age: 24–42
- Played goalkeeper at college or semi-pro level
- Runs private 1-on-1 or small group GK sessions
- Charges $60–$200/hr per session
- Has 8–25 active keeper clients

### Situation
- Needs to justify her rates to parents who are spending $200–500/month on sessions
- Currently sends WhatsApp messages or email updates — no structured reporting
- Wants to look more professional than her competition
- Uses her phone for everything

### Pain Points
- "Parents pay a lot and I need to show them it's working."
- "I spend hours writing session summaries that should take 10 minutes."
- "I have no way to show a keeper's progress over a whole season without digging through old notes."

### Dreams
- Build a reputation as the most professional, data-driven GK trainer in her area
- Charge premium rates and have parents happily pay them
- Scale her client list without drowning in admin work

### Where to Find Her
- **Instagram:** Very active. Posts training clips. Follows @goalkeeper.coach, @gk_performance
- **TikTok:** Posts training reels. Highly engaged with content from other trainers
- **LinkedIn:** Less active but checks for business credibility
- **YouTube:** Watches technique breakdowns for her own development

### Decision Criteria
- "Does this make me look more professional to parents?"
- "Does this save me time on reporting?"
- Price: Higher tolerance — will pay $20–30/mo if it increases her perceived value to clients
- Key objection: "Is this easy enough to use between sessions?"

## ICP 3: The Soccer Parent Who Coaches (Free → Paid Converter)

**Mike, 44. Dad who volunteers as U10 goalkeeper coach.**

### Demographics
- Age: 35–55
- Doesn't have a formal coaching background
- Became the GK coach because nobody else would
- Has a full-time career. Coaches once or twice a week.
- One of his kids is the goalkeeper

### Situation
- Feels out of his depth with goalkeeper-specific development
- Knows he should be doing more but doesn't know what "more" looks like
- Wants to do right by these kids
- Sensitive to parent pressure because he IS a parent

### Pain Points
- "I don't really know what I'm doing and I'm worried I'm not helping enough."
- "Other parents ask me what we're working on and I don't have a good answer."
- "I want to give my goalkeeper real feedback but I don't know what to look for."

### Dreams
- Feel confident he's doing a good job
- Be respected by the other parents
- See the keepers actually improve and know he had something to do with it

### Where to Find Him
- **Facebook:** Very active in local soccer parent groups
- **YouTube:** Searches "how to coach a youth goalkeeper"
- **Google:** "youth goalkeeper coaching tips U10," "what to teach a 9-year-old goalkeeper"

### Decision Criteria
- "Is this going to tell me what to do? I need guidance, not just tracking."
- "Is this easy to use? I don't have time to figure out a complicated app."
- Price: Lowest tolerance — will convert at $10–15/mo if the free tier hooks him first

## Targeting Priority

| ICP | Priority | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Club GK Coach | 🥇 1st | Highest pain, most keepers per coach, best word-of-mouth vector |
| Private GK Trainer | 🥈 2nd | Highest LTV, most likely to advocate publicly (social-native) |
| Parent Coach | 🥉 3rd | Largest TAM but lowest conversion — start with free tier |
