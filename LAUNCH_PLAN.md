# My Keeper Coach — Launch Strategy

This document outlines the two-phase go-to-market strategy for My Keeper Coach, starting with a hyper-focused local club pilot and expanding into a full public launch.

---

## Phase 1: The Local Club Pilot (The "Beta")

**The Goal:** Prove the core value loop with a controlled group, identify friction points in the UX, and extract the marketing assets required for a high-converting public launch.
**The Scope:** 1 Local Club, 2 Coaches, 10 Keepers (and their parents).
**Duration:** 4 Weeks

### Step 1: Onboarding the 2 Coaches
- **The Pitch:** We aren't selling them an app; we are asking them to partner with us to shape the future of keeper development.
- **The Action:** Conduct a 30-minute in-person onboarding. Walk them through the origin story (so they understand the *why*), set up their accounts, create their teams, and load their 10 keepers' profiles together.
- **The Goal:** Ensure they understand the 8 skill pillars and the workflow of the "Live Match Ticker" vs. the post-match detailed review.

### Step 2: Onboarding the Keepers & Parents
- **The Action:** Have the coaches send the invite codes to the 10 keepers and their parents.
- **The Context:** Provide the coaches with a branded email/message template to send to parents. It should explain *why* the club is adopting this tool (focusing on structured development and transparent, positive communication).
- **The Goal:** 100% account activation for the pilot group.

### Step 3: The 4-Week Execution Loop
- **Week 1 (Baseline):** Coaches log 2 training sessions and 1 match for each keeper. Keepers log in and see their first baseline radar chart.
- **Week 2 (The Habit):** Coaches log another week of data. We monitor if they are using the app unprompted and successfully navigating the UI.
- **Week 3 (The Parent Loop):** First major milestones and badges are naturally unlocked. Parents receive their first curated progress updates. We verify the parent emotional response.
- **Week 4 (The Review):** Conduct 1-on-1 exit interviews with the 2 coaches, 2-3 keepers, and 2-3 parents. 

### Step 4: What We MUST Extract Before Public Launch
To execute the GTM strategies (TikTok, Instagram, PR, App Store), we need these specific assets from the Pilot:
1. **The Transformation Story:** Identify the 1 keeper who benefited the most from the visibility. Get their story (with parent permission) to use as our core case study for PR and organic social.
2. **Coach Testimonials:** 2-3 punchy quotes from the pilot coaches on how the app saved them time, organized their assessments, and made them look more professional to parents. (Critical for App Store and Website).
3. **Real UI Data:** Capture screenshots and screen recordings of *real* populated radar charts, match reports, and parent dashboards to replace all placeholder marketing assets.
4. **Friction Points:** Identify the 1-2 things the coaches hated doing in the app (e.g., "it took too many taps to log a save") and fix them before scaling.
5. **Retention Validation:** Did the coaches actually use it for 4 weeks? If not, what is the exact feature drop-off point?

---

## Phase 2: The True Launch

Once the pilot proves that coaches will use the app consistently and parents value the updates, we transition to the public launch using the 8-person GTM team strategies.

### Step 1: The Foundation (Week -2)
- **App Store Optimization:** Update all screenshots with the real data captured from the pilot. Add the coach testimonials to the description. Ensure the keyword field is fully populated.
- **SEO/Web:** Publish the `BRAND_STORY.md` narrative on the website, along with the pilot case study ("How [Local Club Name] Transformed Keeper Development in 4 Weeks").
- **Product Hunt:** Create the launch assets (using the pilot story and real UI recordings as the hook).

### Step 2: The Content Engine (Week -1)
- **Instagram/TikTok:** Begin posting the 30-day content calendar. Lead with educational hooks ("Stop coaching keepers blind").
- **The Social Proof:** Publish a short video showing the "Before and After" of the pilot club out to our social channels.

### Step 3: Launch Day
- **Product Hunt:** Go live at 12:01 AM PST. Activate the 2 pilot coaches, their club network, and our waitlist to upvote and comment early to secure top positioning.
- **PR Push:** Pitch the youth sports media list. Angle: *"How one local dad built an app that is changing how [Local Club Name] develops keepers, and it just launched globally."*
- **Social Core Content:** Post the origin story video on TikTok and Instagram Reels (the *"Dad, am I getting better?"* car ride story). This is the emotional anchor of the launch.

### Step 4: The Growth Loop (Launch + 30 Days)
- **Apple Search Ads:** Turn on brand defense and competitor conquesting campaigns with a small initial budget.
- **Community Engagement:** Respond to every single comment on social and Product Hunt. Gather the first wave of organic feedback.
- **Track Cohorts:** Compare Day 1 and Day 7 retention of the new public users against our pilot group baseline.
