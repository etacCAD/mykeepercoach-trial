## The Problem We Solve

### The Origin Story: The Car Ride Home

It started the way many youth sports companies start: with a dad, a daughter, and a frustrating car ride home.

I didn't play soccer growing up. I came to the sport as a parent of two daughters who both fell in love with it. My youngest decided to become a goalkeeper at age nine. She plays for a club with excellent coaching, but with my background in technology and building structures, I quickly noticed a massive gap.

In youth soccer, the outfield players get all the data. At the end of a game, with modern tech, they know how many passes they made, how much ground they covered, and how many successful tackles they had. 

The goalkeeper? They get virtually no useful data.

I was paying for private, one-on-one sessions—sometimes $100 to $200 an hour—trying to support her. But the feedback loop was broken. One afternoon in the car after a game where they lost late—giving up a tough goal—she looked at me and asked the question that started this entire company:

*"Dad, am I even getting any better?"*

It hit me hard. I couldn't answer her. I couldn't tell her if she was improving. I couldn't tell her if those expensive private sessions were working. I felt like those were really big misses based on how I approach other things in my life and business. 

I looked closer at her team training. The keepers were an absolute afterthought. And it's brutal to see that happening in such an important position with so much stress. 

But it wasn't just about the physical skills. If we aren't tracking development, are we developing their communication? Are we building their leadership? Because to me, the most beautiful and important thing about youth sports isn't just the sport—it's whether they're learning skills that will help them in life. And I felt like keepers were completely underserved in this.

I built My Keeper Coach because I realized I couldn't be the only parent sitting in the car with a kid who just wanted to know if her hard work was paying off. And I knew coaches wanted to help, but they didn't have the tools. We started out just trying to support my daughter better, and it grew into this: a platform to make sure no goalkeeper is ever an afterthought again.

## What My Keeper Coach Actually Does

My Keeper Coach is the **first comprehensive goalkeeper development platform built for the youth game.**

It gives coaches:
- **A structured assessment framework** built around 8 goalkeeper skill pillars, mapped to their keeper's specific age group (U8–U10, U11–U13, U14–U16, U17–U18). No more guessing what to assess. No more one-size-fits-all evaluation.
- **A live match ticker** for quick in-game logging, with a detailed post-match report card that populates automatically.
- **A goalkeeper profile** — a living "GK Profile" — that shows how the keeper is developing across all 8 skill areas over every session, every match, every season.
- **A drill library** with age-specific recommendations — the app tells you what to work on *and* shows you how.
- **Video capture and review** — record technique during training, tag it to specific skills, review in slow motion.
- **Milestone badges** — goalkeeper-specific achievements that celebrate real developmental progress (not just wins).

It gives keepers:
- **Visibility into their own development** — a radar chart showing exactly where they're strong and where to focus.
- **Age-appropriate benchmarks** — a U12 keeper isn't measured against college standards.
- **Self-assessment tools** — the ability to rate their own performance, compare with coach ratings, and identify blind spots.
- **Recognition** — every milestone earned is documented, celebrated, and shareable.

It gives parents:
- **A curated, positive view** of their child's development — milestones, attendance streaks, skill trends, and coach notes. No raw statistics. No pressure-inducing save percentages. Just the story of their child growing.
- **Peace of mind** — knowing their child's coach has a real development framework, not just instinct and good intentions.

## The Problems We Solve (Named)

### Problem 1: Keeper is an Absolute Afterthought

In youth soccer, goalkeepers are often the forgotten player. Team practices are designed for outfield players. The keeper gets 20 minutes of shots at the end of training and calls it a session. It is brutal to see a kid put in such an important, high-stress position and be completely underserved by the training environment.

**We solve this by:** Giving coaches a complete goalkeeper development operating system — from age-appropriate drill selection to skill assessment to progress tracking — so no keeper is ever an afterthought again.

### Problem 2: Coaches Work in the Dark

A dedicated coach can spend 50 sessions with a keeper and still not know, objectively, whether that keeper's footwork has improved. Memory is fallible. Notes get lost. Instinct can't capture trends.

**We solve this by:** Creating a permanent, structured digital record of every session, every assessment, and every milestone — so a coach can open My Keeper Coach before any session and immediately see where their keeper was, where they are now, and where to focus next.

### Problem 3: Age-Inappropriate Development Kills Confidence and Careers

A U9 being drilled on cross-claiming. A U10 being told to dive before their wrists are ready. A U12 seeing their save percentage displayed next to Tier 1 academy benchmarks they can't possibly meet.

This isn't edge-case cruelty. It's the default. Most coaches don't know what they don't know.

**We solve this by:** Age-gating every assessment, benchmark, and drill recommendation in the framework. A U9 will only ever see and practice skills appropriate for their developmental stage. The coach is guided. The keeper is protected.

### Problem 4: Parents Are Left in the Dark (or Given the Wrong Information)

Parents want to know: "Is my child improving? Are they being coached well?" The alternatives are bad:
- Raw save statistics that they'll obsess over out of context
- Vague "they're doing great" feedback that doesn't build trust
- No communication at all — which breeds anxiety

**We solve this by:** The Parent Dashboard — a specifically designed, curated view that shows milestones, attendance streaks, skill trends, and coach notes. Parents see their child's growth as a story, not a spreadsheet.

### Problem 5: Goalkeepers Don't Know Where They Stand

Uniquely in soccer, goalkeepers often have little objective visibility into their own development. Outfield players can count goals, assists, dribbles. Keepers count conceded goals — the unhealthiest possible metric.

**We solve this by:** Giving keepers their own GK Profile — an always-current radar chart showing their development across all 8 skill pillars, compared only to themselves over time, never to peers. Development becomes personal. Progress becomes visible. Motivation becomes intrinsic.

## Our Belief System

These are not marketing statements. These are what we actually believe — and they're reflected in every feature, every design decision, and every word we write.

**1. Great goalkeepers are developed, not discovered.**
Talent is a starting point. Structure, repetition, age-appropriate progression, and encouragement are what turn potential into performance. We built a tool for the *development* side of the equation.

**2. Development is never one-size-fits-all.**
A U8 keeper and a U17 keeper are not just different sizes — they are completely different developmental beings. The same drill, taught the same way, to a U10 vs. a U14, is either too complex to learn from or too simple to grow from. Every feature in the app respects this.

**3. Data should empower, never pressure.**
Statistics in youth sports are a double-edged sword. In the wrong hands, a save percentage number becomes ammunition for a parent argument at a car ride home. In My Keeper Coach, data is always presented in context, always framed for growth, and always protected from misuse. Parents see what helps. Raw data is for coaches only.

**4. Every keeper deserves to feel seen.**
The goalkeeper who saves 3 shots in a 6-0 loss gave everything they had. That deserves recognition. The milestone badge system, the self-assessment tools, and the coach feedback features exist because we believe that a keeper who feels seen and valued stays in the position — and sometimes becomes extraordinary.

**5. The coach is the hero.**
We don't replace goalkeeper coaches. We give them superpowers. The best coaches are already doing the hard work of showing up, building relationships, and teaching the craft. My Keeper Coach gives them structure, memory, communication, and credibility. Their keepers develop faster. Their parents trust them more. Their coaching career grows.

## Audience Profiles

### Primary: The Club Coach
**Who they are:** Youth soccer coach, ages 25–55. Often a parent of a player, a former player, or a dedicated club volunteer. Coaching multiple keepers across one or more teams. Loves the game. Pressed for time. Serious about doing right by their players.

**Their pain:** "I'm giving it my all, but I have no way to show parents what's actually improving. And honestly, I'm not always sure myself if the training is sticking."

**Their dream:** "I want to be the coach whose keepers actually develop — and whose parents trust me completely."

**What My Keeper Coach says to them:** "You've been doing the hard work with no tools. We built this for you."

### Secondary: The Goalkeeper Parent
**Who they are:** Parent of a youth goalkeeper, ages 30–55. Invested in their child's development. Sometimes too invested (pressure anxiety). Genuinely wants what's best. Hungry for context and reassurance.

**Their pain:** "I don't know if my kid is actually improving or if the coach is just being encouraging. And after a loss, I don't know what to say."

**Their dream:** "I want to watch my child fall in love with the position — and know that they're being coached well."

**What My Keeper Coach says to them:** "Your child's coach now has a real development framework. And here's your child's progress story — told the right way."

### Tertiary: The Young Goalkeeper (13–18)
**Who they are:** A teenage goalkeeper who takes their development seriously. Motivated by improvement, competitive in a healthy way. Uses their phone for everything.

**Their pain:** "Nobody tells me where I actually stand. I just know if we won or lost and how many goals I let in."

**Their dream:** "I want to see myself getting better. I want to know I'm on a path."

**What My Keeper Coach says to them:** "Your GK Profile is yours. Watch yourself grow."

## The Emotional Core

At its heart, My Keeper Coach is about **one thing:**

> **Every goalkeeper deserves a coach who sees them clearly — and a tool that helps make that possible.**

Youth goalkeepers are a small, often overlooked group. They stand apart from their teammates by nature of their position. They give everything in a role where a single mistake echoes loudly. And they deserve coaches who are armed with the best possible tools to help them grow.

We built My Keeper Coach because we believe that structured, age-appropriate development — tracked carefully, celebrated genuinely, and shared thoughtfully — changes goalkeeper careers. And sometimes it changes lives.
