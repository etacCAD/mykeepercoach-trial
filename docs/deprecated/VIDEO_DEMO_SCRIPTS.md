# Goalkeeper Drill Video Demo Scripts & Storyboards
### Production Guide for In-App Drill Demonstration Videos

---

## Production Standards

### Video Format
- **Length:** 45–90 seconds per drill (Max: 2 minutes for complex drills)
- **Aspect Ratio:** 9:16 (vertical/portrait) primary for iPhone; 16:9 landscape as secondary
- **Resolution:** 1080p minimum, 4K preferred for zoom/slow-motion
- **Frame Rate:** 60fps (allows clean slow-motion at 50%)

### Visual Style
- Clean, well-lit field or indoor facility
- Keeper wears bright gloves and kit that contrasts with the background
- Equipment (cones, balls) in bright, consistent colors
- Top-down drone angle for field diagrams where possible
- On-screen text overlays for key coaching points

### Audio
- Voiceover narration by a coach (calm, encouraging, authoritative)
- Optional: on-field ambient sound under narration
- No background music during technique explanation (music only on intro/outro)

### On-Screen Elements
- **Skill Pillar badge** in top-left corner
- **Age Group tag** in top-right corner
- **🏠 AT HOME badge** when applicable
- **Equipment checklist** at the start (3 seconds)
- **Slow-motion replay** with freeze-frame annotations for technique points
- **✅ Do / ❌ Don't** split-screen comparisons
- **Progress bar** showing drill phases

---

## Script Template

Every drill video follows this structure:

```
[0:00–0:03]  TITLE CARD
             Drill Name | Skill Pillar | Age Group | 🏠 if solo

[0:03–0:08]  EQUIPMENT CHECK
             Quick visual of everything needed, with labels

[0:08–0:15]  SETUP OVERVIEW
             Drone/wide shot showing the field layout with distances
             Animated lines/arrows overlaid showing positions

[0:15–0:45]  FULL DEMONSTRATION
             Keeper performs the drill at match speed (1 full rep)
             Then slow-motion replay with voiceover coaching cues

[0:45–1:05]  KEY COACHING POINTS (2–3 points)
             Freeze-frame with annotations
             ✅ Do / ❌ Don't split-screen

[1:05–1:15]  COMMON MISTAKES
             Show 1–2 common errors with ❌ overlay,
             then the correction with ✅

[1:15–1:25]  PROGRESSION
             "Once you've mastered this, try..." (harder variation)

[1:25–1:30]  END CARD
             "Track this drill in your Goalie Coach profile"
             Pillar radar chart animation showing where this fits
```

---

## Storyboards by Skill Pillar

### Selection Criteria

I've scripted drills that are the **most visually instructive** and **most frequently used** from each pillar. Priority was given to:
- 🏠 At-home drills (since the video IS the coach for solo practice)
- Technique-heavy drills where visual demonstration is critical
- Drills that span multiple age groups (higher production value ROI)
